What is Color Contrast?

Color contrast ratio is defined by WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) to ensure content is readable for people with visual impairments or color blindness.

WCAG Grade Requirements

  • AA Normal Text: Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio required
  • AA Large Text: Minimum 3:1 ratio (18pt+ or 14pt bold)
  • AAA Normal Text: Minimum 7:1 contrast ratio required
  • AAA Large Text: Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio required

FAQ

Q: How is contrast ratio calculated?

Calculate relative luminance of both colors, then use formula: (lighter + 0.05) / (darker + 0.05)

Q: What's the difference between AA and AAA?

AA is the minimum requirement, AAA is the enhanced standard. Most legal requirements are AA level.

Q: What qualifies as large text?

Text 18pt (24px) or larger, or 14pt (18.5px) bold is considered large text.
