What is a Cron Expression?

A cron expression is a string format used in Unix/Linux systems to define scheduled tasks. It consists of 5 fields representing minute, hour, day of month, month, and day of week. Cron expressions are widely used for server administration, backup automation, periodic data processing, and other scheduling needs.

How to Use

  1. In Visual Builder mode, configure each field (minute, hour, day, month, weekday).
  2. Use preset buttons for quick common configurations.
  3. View the generated expression and human-readable description.
  4. Click the copy button to copy the expression to clipboard.
  5. In Parse Expression mode, enter an existing cron expression to see its meaning.

Supported Syntax

Vixie Cron Standard (Linux/Unix)

  • Month field: numbers (1-12) or names (JAN, FEB, MAR, APR, MAY, JUN, JUL, AUG, SEP, OCT, NOV, DEC)
  • Weekday field: numbers (0-6, 0=Sunday) or names (SUN, MON, TUE, WED, THU, FRI, SAT)
  • Example: 0 9 * * MON-FRI = Weekdays at 9am

Quartz Extensions (Some Systems)

  • L (Last): Last day. In day field, L = last day of month. In weekday field, 5L = last Friday
  • W (Weekday): Nearest weekday. 15W = weekday nearest to the 15th
  • # (Nth): Nth occurrence. 2#3 = third Tuesday
  • Note: These features are not supported in standard Unix cron

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What does * mean?

The asterisk (*) means "every value". For example, * in the minute field means every minute, and * in the hour field means every hour.

Q: What does */5 mean?

The slash (/) indicates an interval. */5 means "every 5 units". In the minute field, */5 runs at 0, 5, 10, 15... minutes.

Q: What does 1-5 mean?

The hyphen (-) indicates a range. In the weekday field, 1-5 means Monday through Friday.

Q: Can I use names like MON or JAN?

Yes, Vixie cron (Linux standard) supports JAN-DEC for months and SUN-SAT for weekdays. Names are case-insensitive.
