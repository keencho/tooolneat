Payment Types Explained

Amortized (Equal Payment)

Pay the same amount (principal + interest) each month. Interest portion is higher initially and decreases over time, while principal portion increases. Easy to budget with consistent monthly payments.

Equal Principal

Pay the same principal amount each month, plus interest on the remaining balance. Initial payments are higher but decrease over time. Results in less total interest than amortized loans.

Interest Only (Bullet)

Pay only interest during the loan term, with the full principal due at maturity. Lowest monthly payments but highest total interest cost.
