What is a Stuck Pixel?

A stuck pixel is a pixel that remains fixed on a particular color (red, green, or blue). Unlike dead pixels which are completely off, stuck pixels may be repairable with software.

How it works: Rapidly changing random colors stimulate the pixel's transistor, encouraging it to return to normal operation. Most stuck pixels recover within 10-20 minutes.

Alternative Repair Methods

Pressure/Massage Method: Gently rub or press the affected area with a soft cloth. This helps spread the liquid crystal. It's more effective when combined with this tool.

Power Off and Rest: Turn off the monitor and leave it for 24+ hours. Problems caused by overuse may naturally recover.

Wait: Stuck pixels often fix themselves over time.

Note: This tool only works on stuck pixels. Physically damaged dead pixels (completely black spots) cannot be repaired with software.

What is a Pixel Fixer?

A pixel fixer (also known as a stuck pixel repair tool) is a software utility designed to repair stuck pixels on LCD and OLED displays. It works by rapidly flashing random colors over the affected area, which stimulates the pixel's transistor and may restore it to normal operation. This non-invasive method is safe for your display and is often the first solution recommended by technicians for stuck pixel issues.

How to Use

  1. First, use the Dead Pixel Test to identify the exact location of stuck pixels
  2. Drag the flashing repair box directly over the stuck pixel location
  3. Adjust the box size if needed to cover the affected area
  4. Run the pixel fixer in fullscreen mode for at least 10-20 minutes
  5. Check results and repeat if necessary; some stubborn pixels need multiple sessions

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long should I run the pixel fixer?

Most stuck pixels require 10-20 minutes of treatment. However, stubborn pixels may need multiple sessions of 30-60 minutes or even overnight treatment. If there is no improvement after several hours of total treatment, the pixel may be permanently dead rather than stuck. Be patient, as some pixels take several days to fully recover.

Q: Will this damage my screen?

No, pixel fixing software is safe for all modern displays. The rapid color changes are similar to what happens during normal video playback, just concentrated in one area. However, avoid running it for extremely extended periods (multiple days continuously) and always allow your monitor to rest. OLED displays should be used with extra caution to prevent burn-in.

Q: What if the pixel fixer does not work?

If software repair fails, the pixel may be dead rather than stuck. You can try the pressure/massage method (gently pressing the affected area with a soft cloth while displaying colors) or leave the monitor off for 24+ hours. If nothing works, the pixel is likely permanently damaged and you may need to consider warranty replacement if the defect is significant.
