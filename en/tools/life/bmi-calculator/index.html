About BMI

  • BMI = Weight (kg) / Height (m)²
  • BMI does not directly measure body fat, so use it as a reference only.
  • Athletes with high muscle mass may have elevated BMI readings.
  • Consult a healthcare professional for accurate health assessment.

What is a BMI Calculator?

A BMI (Body Mass Index) calculator is a health assessment tool that measures body fat based on your height and weight. BMI is widely used by healthcare professionals as an initial screening tool to identify potential weight-related health issues. While BMI does not directly measure body fat, it provides a useful indicator for most people to understand whether they fall within a healthy weight range.

How to Use

  1. Enter your height in centimeters or feet/inches
  2. Enter your current weight in kilograms or pounds
  3. Optionally enter your age and select your gender for more accurate results
  4. Click Calculate to see your BMI value
  5. Review your BMI category (underweight, normal, overweight, or obese)

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a healthy BMI range?

According to the World Health Organization, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered normal weight. Below 18.5 is underweight, 25-29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is classified as obese. However, these ranges may vary slightly for different ethnic groups and should be interpreted alongside other health indicators.

Q: Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI has limitations and may not accurately reflect body composition for athletes with high muscle mass, elderly individuals, pregnant women, or growing children. Muscle weighs more than fat, so muscular individuals may have a high BMI despite being healthy. Always consult a healthcare provider for a comprehensive health assessment.

Q: How often should I check my BMI?

For most adults, checking BMI once every few months is sufficient unless you are actively trying to change your weight. Focus on overall health indicators rather than just BMI. Combine BMI monitoring with other measures like waist circumference, body fat percentage, and regular health check-ups.
