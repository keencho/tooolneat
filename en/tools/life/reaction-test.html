What is a Reaction Time Test?

A reaction time test measures how quickly you can respond to visual stimulus. When the screen color changes, click as fast as possible to measure your reaction time in milliseconds (ms). This test helps you understand your reflexes and compare them with the average human reaction time.

How to Use

  1. Click the blue area to start the test
  2. Wait when the screen turns red ("Wait...")
  3. Click immediately when the screen turns green (after 2-5 seconds)
  4. Your reaction time will be displayed in milliseconds
  5. Complete 5 rounds to see your average reaction time and rating

Rating Scale

Amazing: Under 200ms - Top 1% reaction speed
Good: 200-300ms - Above average reaction speed
Average: 300-400ms - Typical reaction speed
Slow: Over 400ms - Below average reaction speed

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the average human reaction time?

The average human reaction time to visual stimulus is approximately 250ms (0.25 seconds). This includes the time for the signal to travel from your eyes to your brain, for your brain to process the information, and for the command to reach your finger muscles.

Q: Can I improve my reaction time?

Yes, regular practice can improve your reaction time. Adequate sleep, moderate caffeine intake, and concentration training can help. Professional gamers often achieve reaction times of 150-180ms through dedicated training.

Q: Why are there 5 rounds?

A single measurement can be affected by random factors. By taking 5 tests and calculating the average, you get a more accurate and reliable result that better represents your true reaction speed.
